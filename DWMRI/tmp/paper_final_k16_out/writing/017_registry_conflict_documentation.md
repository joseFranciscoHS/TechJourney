## Problem Summary

The `registry.jsonl` has multiple runs with σ=0.1, K=16:

| Job ID | Recipe | Registry line | PSNR (full) | PSNR-ROI | Status |
|--------|--------|---------------|-------------|----------|--------|
| `drcnet_dbrain_rgs_final` | `drcnet_main` | **12** | **23.927** | **26.882** | ✅ **CANONICAL** |
| `drcnet_dbrain_k16_ablation` | `k_sweep` | 26 | 23.878 | 26.909 | Separate K-sweep training |
| `drcnet_dbrain_inference_time_ctx48_pred20` | `inference_time_grid` | 121 | **5.372** | **9.538** | ❌ **CORRUPTED** |

**Root cause**: The `inference_time_grid` jobs used `ctx=48, pred=20` (very high sampling) and wrote metrics to the **same `metrics_dir` paths** as the canonical baselines, overwriting `metrics.json` with values from a different experimental condition.

## Why paper_metrics_summary.csv is Unreliable

The `paper_metrics_summary.csv` was generated by merging metrics from **file paths** (e.g., `metrics/dbrain/.../metrics.json`). Because `inference_time_grid` overwrote those files, the CSV contains **corrupted baseline values**.
